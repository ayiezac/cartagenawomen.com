Tourists from around the world are fascinated with Cartagena de Indias. It is one of the most visited cities in the Americas. From Cartagena's Caribbean climate, beautiful historic Walled City, a good blend of cultures and pristine beaches, and the people, tourists flock to the 'Jewel of the Indies' and make experiences of a lifetime.

5 Reasons Why You Should Travel to Cartagena, Colombia

The people of Cartagena are among the proudest in the world. They are rightful to brag about their city's distinction as a UNESCO world heritage site and their one-of-a-kind culture. More than that, romance, food and dancing are also among the city's pride.

You should not fail to visit Cartagena if you happen to be traveling to Colombia. If you are still not convinced that Cartagena is travel-worthy, here are 5 reasons why Cartagena deserves to be your next travel destination.

1. Gratifying Food

There are varied choices of Colombian food in every region, and all of it is delicious. You may find your favorite food in one of the cities of Colombia --- most likely in Cartagena.

One of the many things tourists look forward to when visiting Cartagena is the food. No one hates food in any way. The walled city plays host to an abundance of talented chefs who play a big part in the city's thriving tourism. There is something mouthwatering that costs in every single budget, whether fine dining at high-end eateries serving seafood or local market stalls serving delicious stews along the streets. There should be no reason for you not to try Cartagena's very own cuisine.

2. Distinctive Culture

When you stroll around one of Cartagena's plazas, there is a big chance for you to see the iconic palenqueras, they are Afro-Colombian women in bright dresses selling fruits. There are a good number of them in the UNESCO-recognized village of San Jose Basilio de Palenque, where they originally came from.

Not only is Cartagena the home of wonderful art, literature, and architecture, it is also the best place to visit when you want to experience a truly distinct kind of Cartagena culture and traditions.

3. Old and New

The juxtaposition of the ancient and the modern is evident in many aspects of Cartagena. The walls of the old city and the tower blocks and hotel chains of Bocagrande are an ever-present reminder that, despite the city's modern developments, it has kept its humble beginnings.

In Cartagena, you will concretely see the contrast between old and new. When you are into the treasures of the past, there is certainly a place for you here. If you are, on the other hand, for modern luxuries, it is just everywhere.

This is part of Cartagena's unique charm. Something that you would not experience in any place of its kind.

4. Art Everywhere

No one can help, but fall in love with the beauty of art. If art is on your list to discover in your next trip, Cartagena should be on top of your destinations. It is an artistic and creative city where you will deliberately see excellent street art and murals especially in the district of Getsemani, and a place of fine local artists whose works are on display at the Museum of Modern Art.

The Old City of Cartagena keeps a wide range of galleries that even the less artistically inclined will surely be impressed by the wonderful statues and sculptures like Fernando Botero's famous Gertrudis statue. While some amateur artists are selling paintings and drawings at the plazas, you will also be amazed by how these personals display their culture through arts.

Fall in love with the beauty of art and the artistic people of Cartagena.

5. A Huge Room for Romance

Cartagena is one of the settings of a world-renowned novel written by a Colombian writer, Gabriel Garcia Marquez's Love in the Time of Cholera. The novel has burned the fire of romance among many couples, making Cartagena an ideal city for weddings, honeymoons, and romantic escapades. Sitting in the city walls at sunset, sipping cocktails at Cafe del Mar, checking in one of the myriad romantic boutique hotels and intimate restaurants, there is no other way for you to find a romantic city like Cartagena.

The romantic people live in this romantic city. Many single foreign men join in romance tours to Cartagena to meet stunning Cartagena women.

Cartagena is the fifth-largest city in Colombia. It lies on the northern coast of the country in the Caribbean Coast Region. It faces the Caribbean Sea to the west. To the south is the Cartagena Bay where there are two entrances: Bocachica (Small Mouth) in the south, and Bocagrande (Big Mouth) in the north.

In the history of Cartagena, it was a key port for the export of Peruvian silver to Spain and for the import of enslaved Africans under the asiento system during the colonial era. It was backed then the center of political, religious, and economic activities. The walled city and fortress were designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1984. At present, it plays a major role in Colombia's tourism industry, logistics, and commerce.
