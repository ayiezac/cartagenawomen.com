How Cartagena Became Colombia’s MUST SEE Destination

Find out 5 reasons why you should visit Cartagena, Colombia. | Photo by Ricardo Gomez Angel on Unsplash

If you’re thinking, however, that that’s all Cartagena has to offer, you’d be gravely mistaken. There are tons of other tourist attractions and exciting activities to be had in the city.

Below are 5 often overlooked reasons to visit Cartagena, Colombia:

  • A fusion of old and new

    • While Cartagena is considered a modern city, there are some areas that look and feel like it’s frozen in time. Places like the UNESCO-protected Old Town and Bocagrande still feel like they’re stuck in the Spanish-colonization era.

    But when you head into the city, you feel like you’re back in modern civilization. In a way, it feels like you can travel back in time.

    The fact that they are paving the way forward but still keeping in touch with the past shows how much the city values its heritage.

  • Colorful fixtures

    • Cartagena is one of the most colorful cities in Colombia. Not only do they have vibrant plants and flowers, but they also paint their houses and landmarks in vibrant blues, reds, and greens.

    A lot of the surviving Spanish era architecture is painted with vivid and vibrant colors making the colorful aesthetic of the city very pleasing to the eyes.

  • Quirky rides

    • Getting around the city is just as memorable and exciting as the sights and attractions are pretty.

    When you travel around the city, make sure to board some Chiva buses. They were used for public transport, however, now they are just used for select tours.

    Some of the unique features of the Chiva bus include a side ladder, pew-like seats, and the sound system for the tour guide. You definitely won’t forget getting on board this bus.

  • Seafood heaven

    • While there are plenty of dishes worth trying, you should especially try the seafood. Expect them not only to be savory, but fresh as well.

    Due to Cartagena’s location which faces the Caribbean Sea, one of the city’s major industries is fishing. Thus, it comes as no surprise that seafood is the highlight of the city’s cuisine.

    Two of the most famous dishes in the city include Cazuela de Mariscos and Camarones al Ajillo. They also have their own version of Ceviche, which is a common South American seafood salad.

  • Gorgeous beaches

    • Your Cartagena trip would not be complete if you don’t visit the world-famous Cartagena beaches. And who would resist this jewel of the Caribbean?

    Some of the more popular beaches are found in Bocagrande and La Boquilla. But you can also hit the waves of Playa Blanca, Isla Grande, and Tierra Bomba.

    Just imagine yourself sipping on your margarita while lounging on a white sand beach watching the sun sets into the horizon.

While we can list down the many great things about Cartagena, there’s nothing like experiencing the sights and sounds yourself. If you decide to visit, it’ll be one of the most memorable places you’ll ever get to experience.
